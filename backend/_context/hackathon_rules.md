WEILLIPTIC HACKATHON - XPECTO’26
Event Overview:
The Weilliptic Hackathon is a forward-looking builder event inviting developers, designers, and innovators to shape the future of Web4 on the Weilchain platform. Participants will explore, experiment, and create intelligent, decentralized applications by leveraging the Weilliptic technology stack and the Icarus agentic chatbot.
This hackathon challenges builders to go beyond traditional Web3 paradigms and design next-generation applets, DApps, MCPs, and autonomous agents that are intelligent, composable, and deeply interactive. By combining agentic AI, decentralized infrastructure, and novel on-chain/off-chain orchestration, participants will prototype applications that feel alive, adaptive, and user-centric.
Teams and individuals are encouraged to creatively integrate Icarus, Weilliptic’s agentic chatbot, to enable reasoning, automation, and conversational intelligence across their solutions. Whether it’s autonomous agents coordinating on-chain actions, MCPs orchestrating complex workflows, or applets that seamlessly blend AI and decentralization, the hackathon is a space to push boundaries and reimagine what decentralized applications can become.
Over the course of the event, participants will receive access to tools, documentation, and mentorship from the Weilliptic ecosystem. The hackathon culminates in project demos evaluated on innovation, technical execution, agentic intelligence, and real-world impact—with winning teams gaining recognition, prizes, and opportunities to continue building within the Weilchain ecosystem.
The Weilliptic Hackathon is more than a competition—it’s an invitation to co-create the foundations of Web4, where intelligent agents, decentralized systems, and human creativity converge.

General Guidelines
Hackathon Domains
The hackathon is intentionally domain-agnostic. Participants are free to build across any domain or use case, as long as the project is developed on the Weilchain platform and makes meaningful use of the Weilliptic technology stack and/or the Icarus agentic chatbot. Creativity, originality, and thoughtful application of the ecosystem are strongly encouraged.
Allowed Resources
Everything is allowed.
Participants may use:
Open-source and closed-source libraries


SDKs, APIs, frameworks, and tooling


AI assistants and LLMs (including vibe-coding workflows)


Any development environment or supporting technology


There are no restrictions on tools or resources, provided participants comply with applicable licenses and legal requirements.
That said, participants are encouraged to rely heavily on official Weilliptic documentation, as the tech stack is relatively new and may not yet be well-represented in existing LLM training data. In previous hackathons, we’ve observed that over-reliance on LLM-generated code sometimes slowed development due to inaccurate or non-existent components (e.g., incorrect WIDL files). Documentation and hands-on experimentation will often be the most reliable path forward.
Plagiarism & Originality
All submissions must represent original work created during the hackathon.


Use of existing libraries, frameworks, and open-source components is fully permitted, but teams must clearly disclose third-party dependencies and give appropriate attribution where required.


Directly copying or submitting existing projects without meaningful modification or innovation is not allowed.


Freedom to Build
There are no hard constraints on how you build. Participants are free to use any tools, workflows, or approaches that help them create impactful, high-quality applications. The goal is to empower builders to explore, experiment, and deliver exceptional Weilliptic-powered applications.
We strongly encourage participants to join the official Discord community and share their questions in the relevant channels from the very beginning of the project—even before submitting an idea. The Weilliptic engineering team will be more than happy to provide guidance, clarify expectations, and help shape the types of applications participants may develop.
Some Ideas
Multi-Step Agentic Workflows on Icarus
Overview
Design and implement a sophisticated, multi-step agentic workflow using the Weil-SDK and the Model Context Protocol (MCP). The workflow should seamlessly integrate multiple external services and APIs, reflecting real-world automation scenarios—such as CockroachDB with Zendesk or Gmail with Calendar—while extending into novel and creative use cases.
Challenge
Build an intelligent agentic system that:
Orchestrates multiple external services using only Weilliptic’s SDK for third-party integrations


Interprets and executes complex, multi-step workflows driven by natural language instructions


Deploys and visualizes workflows on Icarus, enabling transparent, end-to-end execution tracking


Preserves on-chain auditability and enforces policy constraints throughout the entire workflow lifecycle


Manages data flow across service boundaries, including robust error handling, recovery mechanisms, and conditional branching

Problem Statement 2: On-Chain Applet DApp
Overview
Design and build a decentralized application (DApp) that allows users to discover, invoke, and interact with applets deployed on the WeilChain blockchain. The goal is to create a thriving ecosystem where on-chain applets function as discoverable, composable, and monetizable services.
Challenge
Develop a marketplace-style DApp that:
Provides an intuitive, user-friendly interface for discovering and browsing deployed applets


Enables users to invoke applets using wallet-based authentication and seamless transaction management


Facilitates value exchange between users and applet providers through secure blockchain transactions


Records execution history and results on-chain to ensure transparency and auditability


Supports applet composition, allowing users to chain multiple applets into complex workflows


Introduces innovative monetization models or incentive mechanisms to encourage adoption and high-quality applet development


Inspiration & Example Use Cases
AI Task Marketplace: Users purchase tokenized AI services—such as data analysis or model inference—executed as on-chain applets


Smart Contract Audit Services: On-demand security analysis of smart contracts, with audit results permanently stored on-chain


Oracle Services: Applets that fetch, verify, and attest to external data, minting NFTs as proof of service


Agentic Commerce: Autonomous agents that execute transactions and business logic on behalf of users

The ideas above are provided purely for inspiration. We strongly encourage teams to explore creative variations—or propose entirely novel concepts—that push the boundaries of what can be built on WeilChain.
